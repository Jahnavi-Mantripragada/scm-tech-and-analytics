## The "Category Bias" Myth

I initially suspected that low retention was driven by the sale of **one-off durable goods**.  
The data **contradicts** this assumption.

Both **repeat** and **one-time** buyers shop in the **same core categories**  
*(Bed/Bath, Health & Beauty, etc.)*.

However, **repeat buyers spend significantly less**  
*(~$102 vs. ~$121)*.

### The Real Insight
The true **leaky bucket driver** is not category mix, but the combination of:
- **High price friction**, and  
- A lack of **habit-forming UX**

Customers return for **low-stakes replenishment**, not high-commitment purchases.
